Pediatric Cancer

A Devestating Disease

Pediatric Cancer is the deadliest disease in the U.S., killing almost 1,780 children anually. Over 20% of children diagnosed with cancer in the U.S. do not survive, and pediatric cancers tend to be more agressive and deadly than those experienced by adults.

A Coordinated Research Effort

In children ages 0 to 19, the most common cancers are often much more deadly than the avarage adult cancer, and as such, more research is required to find effective treatments while minimizing harmful side effects. To improve the effectiveness of research, researchers around the globe must collaborate in order to share information and findings.
